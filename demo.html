Future Implementation

Breathing Cycle Vibration Mode

  • Objective: To help users manage stress and anxiety through controlled breathing simulations.

  • Implementation: Integrate a vibration motor in Bob that adjusts its vibration patterns to imitate the natural rhythm of breathing.

  • Control: Activation via a button on Bob or an app interface.

Anxiety Sensor Integration

  • Objective: To detect signs of elevated stress or anxiety in the user and provide timely interventions.

  • Implementation: Equip Bob with a physiological sensor, such as a galvanic skin response (GSR) sensor, which measures the electrical conductance of the skin. Changes in this parameter can indicate stress.

  • Functionality: When elevated levels of stress or anxiety are detected, Bob can automatically switch to the breathing cycle vibration mode or alert the user to take a break from their study cycle, instead of strictly implementating 30 minutes study timer.

Techinical implementation

We have observed an unstable connection in our current soldered circuit, particularly after soldering the switch circuit twice without achieving the desired functionality. For future implementations, we are considering switching to electrically conductive thread to construct sewing circuits instead of using soldered wires. This approach is expected to provide better connectivity and reliability.
